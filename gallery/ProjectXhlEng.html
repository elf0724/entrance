ProjectX Historical Life

    There was an article published by San Francisco Chronicle on May 8, 2011 which is named "Exploring Tainan, Taiwan's hometown - Spud Hilton, Chronicle Travel Editor, Sunday, May 8, 2011, San Francisco Chronicle". It introduced Tainan as a historical city in Taiwan. Mr. Spud Hilton who is the editor of San Francisco Chronicle concluded all in one words that "History and tradition here, it seemed, are not meant just for watching." after his personal traveling in Tainan.  In my hometown - Tainan, history is not just for watching but really presented as an inheritance in our daily life. It obvious that people retained many traditional customs of local life from everywhere you can see to all festivals. And through the vision of a foreign news editor who had been traveled here personally and seen the lifestyle of local people, I believe it could reflect the fact in certain degree of objectivity from the conclusion which was wrote by him.

    Since I entered the field of photography, the historic sites of Tainan are the main subject I've been shot. In the process of photo taking, I've accumulated some observation of the interaction between the historic sites and people live in Tainan in few years. Compared to the attitude that retaining or isolating historic sites rigidly just because of fearing of the damage to them, It's very lively in the preservation and application of historic sites whether in government or private areas. It can be said that the life of people in Tainan is inseparable from historic sites, and not only that, it could even be a very close combination. You can see the ordinary folk blend in with the historic sites harmony in food, clothing, housing, transportation, education, joy and so on in the life of general people. The author will present images which were captured based on the realization described above in this article, and the topic of it is "Historical Life".
